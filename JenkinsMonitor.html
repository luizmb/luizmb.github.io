Jenkins Monitor

for Apple TV

What? Why?

When I was working at HERE Maps, every room had a TV with an Apple TV connected so we could use AirPlay to make app presentations or share the screen of a Macbook during the Daily or Planning.

These TVs also had a Raspberry Pi connected to another HDMI port and showing the Jenkins Monitor in a browser during the day, so we could quickly monitor when a build was broken.

In my free time I decided to develop this simple Jenkins Monitor app so we could get rid of the Raspberry Pi (and its keyboard + mouse) and use exclusively the Apple TV.

Technical Details

This is a very simple app, developed in a couple of hours and has absolutely no intention to be robust or fail-proof.

It doesn't use good practices or has any tests, the intention was solving a specific problem using the least amount of my private time as possible. It was also my first Apple TV app, so learning a bit of tvOS in the process was fun.

The app ran for at least a couple of weeks in our office room, but as I was leaving HERE Maps at that point, I don't know for how long it was used.

Later the repository was forked and another developer added Travis support, and also a new design.

It was a fun project to make while learning tvOS, it could be useful too. It's certainly not mission-critical but hopefully it proves that a pragmatic approach sometimes is enough to accomplish small goals in a very limited amount of time.
